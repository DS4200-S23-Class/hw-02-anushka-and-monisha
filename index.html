Task Abstraction

High Level: Discover (the visualization is finding some new revelation about current weather in Vancouver)

Medium Level: Locate (the viusalization knows the target of the past 3 days to look for but the location on the graph was originally unknown)

Low Level: Identify (the visualization has a single target of the most recent points from the past three days for users to look for)

Target: All Data - Outliers (the visualization utilizes all its data but focuses specifically on outliers in the charted change of seasonal Vancouver temperatures)

High Level: Present (the visualization is depicting results of a specific domain to prove a point rather than exploring the data for a new discovery)

Medium Level: Browse (the visualization has no clear targets but the user knows the locations of when there is visual bars on specific years)

Low Level: Summarize (the users can observe all targets rather than having specific targets in mind)

Target: One Attribute - Extremes (the visualization focuses on the specific attribute of the number of times the daily mean temperature exceeded a certain threshold of normal and the extremes which are the years with most times this attributes is recorded is the data of interest to the user)
